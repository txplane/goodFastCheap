About the Project Triangle

This principle, also known as the Iron Triangle, is fundamental in project management. It illustrates that project constraints are interconnected - improving one aspect typically requires compromising another.

Real-World Applications:
  • Software Development: Feature-rich apps vs. quick MVPs vs. budget constraints
  • Manufacturing: Premium materials vs. production speed vs. cost efficiency
  • Services: Expert consultants vs. tight deadlines vs. budget limitations
